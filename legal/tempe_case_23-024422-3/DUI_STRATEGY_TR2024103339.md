## Phase 1: Kill the Warrant

Nothing moves until the bench warrant is quashed. You cannot negotiate, plea, or do anything while it's active.

### Step 1: Call the Clerk
- **El Centro Justice Court:** (602) 372-6300
- Tell them: "I have case TR2024103339. I'm an out-of-state defendant in Michigan. I want to resolve my FTA and get back on the calendar. What do I need to file?"
- **Ask specifically:**
  - Can I file a Motion to Quash by mail or electronically?
  - Does the court accept telephonic/virtual appearances for misdemeanors?
  - Is there a self-help form for motions to quash?
- **Be polite. Be cooperative. You're not running — you're trying to come back.**

### Step 2: File a Motion to Quash the Warrant
Maricopa County has an actual **pro se form** for this:
- **Form:** CRMQ11f — "Motion to Quash Warrant and Request a Hearing Date"
- **Download:** https://superiorcourt.maricopa.gov/media/mrppvpam/crmq11fz.pdf
- Note: This is a Superior Court form. The clerk will tell you if El Centro Justice Court uses the same form or has its own. Ask them.

**What to write in the motion (key points):**
1. I currently reside in Michigan and cannot travel to Arizona
2. I was unaware of the arraignment date / could not attend due to being out of state
3. I am not evading the court — I am proactively seeking to resolve this case
4. I respectfully request the warrant be quashed and a new hearing date be set
5. I request permission to appear telephonically or via video

### Step 3: Request Telephonic Appearance
Arizona courts routinely allow telephonic appearances for misdemeanors. File a **Motion to Appear Telephonically** alongside the Motion to Quash. Key language:

> "Defendant resides in Wayland, Michigan and is financially unable to travel to Arizona. Defendant respectfully requests to appear by telephone/video for all proceedings in this matter pursuant to Arizona Rule of Criminal Procedure 1.5."

**Arizona Rule of Criminal Procedure 1.5** explicitly allows courts to conduct proceedings by "audio-visual" or "telephonic" means. This is your legal basis.

## Phase 2: Negotiate the Charge Down

Once the warrant is quashed and you're back on the calendar, you're in a position to negotiate. Here's what you're working with:

### What Arizona Law Says (ARS 28-1381, First Offense)
If convicted of a standard first-offense DUI, mandatory minimums are:
- **10 days in jail** (but 9 can be suspended if you complete alcohol screening)
- **$1,250+ in fines** (with surcharges it's closer to $2,000-2,500)
- **90-day license suspension** in Arizona
- **Alcohol screening and treatment**
- **Community service**
- **Ignition interlock** on any Arizona-registered vehicle

### Your Angle: The "Conforming Reckless" Plea
Arizona has a specific plea deal called a **"Conforming Reckless"** (aka "wet reckless"). This is a reckless driving conviction that carries the **same penalties as a DUI** but is NOT a DUI on your record. 

**Why this matters for you:**
- A reckless driving conviction does NOT carry the same NDR weight as a DUI
- It may not trigger the same automatic blocks for your Michigan DAAD hearing
- Prosecutors offer this when the DUI case has weaknesses

### Weaknesses to Identify in Your Case
You need to figure out what the prosecution actually has. Call the clerk and request:
1. **The police report / complaint** — you're entitled to a copy
2. **The toxicology results** — what substances, what levels
3. **Any body cam or dash cam footage** — request through discovery

**Potential weaknesses to look for:**
- Was there a valid reason for the initial contact? (no traffic stop = potential 4th Amendment issue)
- How long between the incident and the blood/urine draw? (chain of custody issues)
- The 3-month gap between incident (Oct 2023) and filing (Jan 2024) — were there documentation issues?
- Were you in "actual physical control" of a vehicle, or was there some other circumstance?

### What to Say to the Prosecutor
Once you know who the assigned prosecutor is, email them directly. You've already done this successfully with Alan Witt. Here's the approach:

> Subject: TR2024103339 — Kerr — Request for Resolution
>
> My name is Jeff Kerr. I'm the defendant in case TR2024103339. I currently reside in Michigan and am unable to travel to Arizona. I have filed a Motion to Quash the bench warrant and am seeking to resolve this case remotely.
>
> I am representing myself pro se and would like to discuss a plea resolution. I am willing to accept responsibility and complete any required treatment/classes remotely in Michigan.
>
> I would appreciate the opportunity to discuss this by phone or email at your convenience. My number is [PHONE]. 
>
> Respectfully,
> Jeff Kerr

**Key negotiation points:**
- You're a first-time DUI offender in Arizona (your Michigan OWIs from 1999/2000 are 25+ years old)
- You're not a flight risk — you're proactively reaching out
- You'll do whatever treatment/classes they want, but from Michigan
- You want to close this case as much as they do
- A plea to reckless driving or a reduced charge saves them a trial they'll never get because you're never coming back

## Phase 3: The DAAD Angle

### Best-Case Scenario
Prosecutor agrees to reduce charges to reckless driving or some non-DUI offense. You plead by mail/phone, pay fines on a payment plan, complete treatment in Michigan. The warrant is cleared, the case is closed, and the NDR hit is minimized for your DAAD hearing.

### Acceptable Scenario
You plead guilty to the DUI by mail, get the mandatory minimums. Arizona suspends your AZ driving privileges (which you don't have anyway). The jail time may be convertible to home detention or community service — and since you're out of state, the court may waive or modify that requirement. The case is CLOSED, the warrant is GONE, and you can proceed with DAAD.

### The Nuclear Option (if they won't work with you)
If the prosecutor and judge refuse to let you appear remotely and insist you come to Arizona, you have a choice:
- Let the warrant sit. Arizona will NOT extradite on a misdemeanor DUI. Michigan will not arrest you on an out-of-state misdemeanor warrant during a routine stop (generally). But it stays on the NDR and blocks your DAAD permanently.
- Find a way to get to Arizona, walk into the court, deal with the warrant (you may be held briefly), and resolve it in person.
